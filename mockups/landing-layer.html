Platform thesis

WordPress already has the ingredients. AssemblyPress gives them a community model.

Identity

Profiles, fields, directories, and editable forms are the first proof of the core community loop.

Discussion

Forums, topics, spaces, replies, and notifications are planned as coherent community primitives.

Health

Moderation, permissions, visibility, and review states belong in the same product model.

Native surfaces

Public pages are block-native. Management is DataViews-native.

Site Editor

Community pages should be shaped with blocks, patterns, templates, and theme-aware styling.

DataViews

Members, spaces, discussions, reports, and moderation queues should use modern WordPress collection views.

Patterns

Designers should be able to translate community work into native templates and patterns.

Follow the open build.

AssemblyPress is early, intentional, and built in the open for people who want WordPress to be the best place to create owned communities again.
